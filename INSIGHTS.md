## Title: Level Design UX Insights - February Observation Period

Using the LILA Journey Tool, the following gameplay behaviors were identified:

Insight 1: High-Friction "Choke Point" at Ambrose Valley Bridge
Observation: The Global Heatmap shows a dense cluster of Red X (Bot Kills) and Blue Triangles (Human Deaths) at the central bridge crossing.

Analysis: Players are forced into a bottleneck during Storm shrinks. The high death rate here suggests that the current "Cover-to-Loot" ratio is too low for a safe crossing.

Design Recommendation: Add a series of "wrecks" or boulders in the riverbed to allow for a lower-visibility flanking route beneath the bridge.

Insight 2: Bot Pathing Immersion Break
Observation: When filtering for Bot Paths (Orange lines), movements are strictly linear (A to B) with no "zig-zag" looting behavior.

Analysis: This makes bots extremely easy to identify and "farm" for human players, potentially devaluing the combat experience.

Design Recommendation: Introduce "Loot Points of Interest" in the Bot AI pathing nodes to force them into buildings, making them behave more like human players.

Insight 3: The "Grand Rift" Loot Trap
Observation: There is a high density of Gold Diamonds (Loot Events) in the lower valley of Grand Rift, but very few surviving paths (Blue lines) exiting the area.

Analysis: Players are being lured into a low-ground area with poor extraction routes.

Design Recommendation: Either increase the quality of loot to justify the high risk or add a "Gravity Lift" / Zip-line to allow players a quick vertical exit from the valley.
